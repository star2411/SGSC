SURGICAL PROCEDURES

  1. SLeeve Gastrectomy: This removes 80% of the stomach, leaving a banana shaped pouch.
    This reduces hunger hormones and limits food intake
    • Pros: Significant weight loss, shorter hospital stay, and no intestinal rerouting.
    • Cons: Non-reversible, potential for vitamins deficiencies, risk of acid reflux.

  2. Roux-en-Y Gastric Bypass: Creates a small stomach pouch and reroutes the small intestine.
    • Pros: Effective for weight loss and diabetes management, with potantial for long term remission.
    • Cons: More complex surgery, potential for vitamin deficiencies, and risk of complications.

  3. Adjustable gastric banding (AGB): Limiting food intake and helping individuals feel full sooner.
    It is an effective option for those struggling with obesity, offering a less invasive solutions with potentially fewer complications.
